Foothills Middle School, Arcadia CA

Research on Foothills Middle School — a 2022 National Blue Ribbon School in Arcadia Unified School District

641 Students Enrolled
63% Asian Students
2022 Blue Ribbon Year

Corroborated Findings (both sources agree)

Basic Facts

  • Public middle school serving grades 6–8 in Arcadia, California.
  • Part of the Arcadia Unified School District (AUSD).
  • Located at 171 East Sycamore Ave., Arcadia, CA 91006.
  • Website: fh.ausd.net.
  • (Sources: grok + perplexity)

Enrollment & Demographics

  • Enrollment: approximately 641–684 students (Grok: 641 CDE 2025-26, ~650-684 other sources; Perplexity: ~684).
  • Majority Asian: ~63–64% (Grok: 63-64%; Perplexity: 429 students, ~63%).
  • Hispanic/Latino: ~18% (Grok: 18%; Perplexity: 128 students, ~19%).
  • White: ~8–11% (Grok: 8-11%; Perplexity: 73 students, ~11%).
  • Two or More Races: ~6–7% (Grok: 6-7%; Perplexity: 47 students, ~7%).
  • Small percentages Black/African American, American Indian, Pacific Islander.
  • (Sources: grok + perplexity)

National Blue Ribbon School

  • Named a 2022 National Blue Ribbon School by the U.S. Department of Education — one of few in California — recognizing strong overall academic performance and success in closing achievement gaps.
  • (Sources: grok + perplexity)

Academic Performance

  • Historically high-performing with strong proficiency rates.
  • API of 977 (2012–2013) — Perplexity; Grok confirms long history of top API rankings (#1 tied among LA County middle schools on older measures).
  • Strong CAASPP/Smarter Balanced performance: ~70–75% proficient in math, ~75% in reading/ELA, well above state averages.
  • (Sources: grok + perplexity)

Gifted & Talented Program

  • Offers a Gifted & Talented (GATE) program.
  • (Sources: grok + perplexity)

Accreditation & AUSD Context

  • AUSD ranks in the top 1% of U.S. school districts according to Niche.
  • All AUSD schools recognized as California Distinguished Schools.
  • (Sources: grok + perplexity)

Facilities

  • Includes classrooms, science labs, music building, multipurpose room, library, computer lab.
  • Part of past bond-funded improvements.
  • (Sources: grok + perplexity)

Additional from Grok Search

Leadership

  • Principal: Dr. Benjamin Acker.

Enrollment Detail

  • Student-teacher ratio: ~24:1.
  • ~29 full-time teachers.
  • Gender split: ~48% female, 52% male.
  • ~29% economically disadvantaged.
  • ~8.6% English Learners (~55 students; Perplexity says 62 / 9.1% — close but slightly different).

Rankings (Recent)

  • Niche: A+ overall, A in academics; #34 Best Public Middle Schools in California (out of ~2,749); #29 in CA for best public middle school teachers.
  • U.S. News & World Report: #107 in California middle schools; #2–3 within Arcadia USD; overall score 95.33/100.
  • Chronic absenteeism: Low; daily attendance ~95%.

Programs & Extracurriculars

  • AVID (Advancement Via Individual Determination): College-readiness elective supporting motivated students in the academic middle.
  • District-wide initiatives: Mental health support (full-time counselors), "The Leader in Me" character education, restorative practices, social-emotional learning.
  • Student-driven clubs (change yearly; students propose via application): Anime Club, Art Journaling/Calligraphy, AVID Club, Board Games, Chess & Checkers, Colorful Artists, Cup Stacking, Curious Scientists, DIY, Foothills Broadcast Club, Green Club, Mystery Club, Open Mic, Tabletop Games.
  • Electives: Arts, music, STEM, physical education, leadership, broadcast/media, science-oriented activities.
  • Athletics: Offered as part of physical development focus and AUSD offerings (specific teams vary).

Reviews

  • Niche reviews average ~3.6/5 — mixed but generally positive on academics and teachers; some note competitiveness.
  • Teachers receive A+ grade on Niche for care and classroom management; high teacher salaries noted.

Additional from Perplexity

Historical Academic Milestones

  • 2007–2008: Reported as 2nd in API among California public school systems and the 7th best middle school in California.
  • More than 90% of students scored in advanced proficient category for ELA, Math, and Science in historical reports.

Facilities — Fault Line Discovery

  • A construction inspection reportedly found the school was built on a large fault, which impacted remodeling efforts under the Measure I bond improvement plan (2000s).
  • (Not mentioned by Grok — unique and notable finding.)

District Founding Date

  • Perplexity attributes AUSD's founding to 1952. (Grok does not specify district founding year in its findings.)

Community Involvement

  • National Blue Ribbon recognition brought significant community attention and pride.
  • School mission emphasizes students making a "positive and profound impact on their world."

CONFIDENCE: High

Both sources are closely aligned on all major dimensions: identity (grades 6-8, AUSD, 171 E. Sycamore), enrollment (~640–685), demographics (majority Asian ~63%, Hispanic ~18%, White ~10%), National Blue Ribbon (2022), strong academic performance (~70-75% math/reading proficiency, historical 977 API), and Gifted & Talented program. Grok provides significantly richer detail — principal name, rankings, test scores, student-teacher ratio, AVID, mental health initiatives, and an extensive club list. Perplexity corroborates the core facts and adds one unique facility finding (fault line discovery). Minor variances in enrollment and English Learner counts are attributable to different data snapshots and are not material contradictions.

GAPS

  • Fault line impact: Perplexity mentions the school was found to be built on a large fault that impacted remodeling — but no details on resolution, safety implications, or current status. This is a potentially significant facilities concern that neither source investigates further.
  • Athletics detail: Grok mentions athletics are offered but provides no specific sports, teams, or competitive results. Perplexity provides none.
  • Music program detail: Both sources mention music facilities (music building) but no specifics on band, orchestra, choir, or performance achievements. Given the strong music program at feeder school Arcadia High, the middle school music pipeline is an important gap.
  • Feeder pattern detail: Foothills feeds into Arcadia High School, but neither source explicitly discusses the transition, articulation, or college-readiness pipeline from middle to high school.
  • Club completeness: Grok provides a club list but notes it's student-driven and changes yearly — the list is illustrative, not comprehensive for any given year.
  • Teacher quality metrics: Niche grades are cited (A+ for teachers) but no specific teacher retention data, credential breakdown, or diversity statistics.
  • COVID impact: No discussion of pandemic-era remote learning, learning loss, or recovery efforts — notable for a high-performing school with English Learners and economically disadvantaged students.
  • Competitive academic teams: Unlike Arcadia High School (which has extensive Academic Decathlon, Science Olympiad, etc.), neither source documents whether Foothills fields competitive academic teams.
  • School-specific history: No founding date for Foothills specifically — Perplexity notes AUSD was founded in 1952 but that may be the district, not the school. The actual opening date of Foothills Middle School is not documented in either source.
  • No school-level funding data: Per-pupil spending at the school level not provided.
